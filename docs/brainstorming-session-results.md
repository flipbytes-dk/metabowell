## Executive Summary

### Topic
**Femwell - Comprehensive Strategic Planning Session**

Femwell is a women's hormonal health platform launching in India, targeting Tier 1 and Tier 2 cities. The platform combines teleconsultations, diagnostics, and lifestyle guidance focused on menopause, hormone replacement therapy (HRT), and medical weight loss (GLP-1 medications).

### Session Goals
- Explore overall platform strategy
- Develop marketing and customer acquisition approaches  
- Define features and product roadmap
- Identify partnership opportunities
- Create go-to-market strategy for Tier 1 vs Tier 2 cities
- Address regulatory compliance concerns
- Determine optimal technology stack (PWA focus)

### Techniques Used
1. **What If Scenarios** (20 min) - Expansive exploration
2. **Role Playing** (20 min) - Multi-stakeholder perspectives
3. **SCAMPER Method** (25 min) - Structured feature innovation
4. **Assumption Reversal** (15 min) - Risk identification
5. **Time Shifting** (10 min) - Technology and timing decisions

### Total Ideas Generated
**57+ actionable ideas** across platform features, business strategy, partnerships, and market positioning

### Key Themes Identified:
- **Simplicity as Strategy** - Relatable language, visual-first design, 3-tap UX rule
- **Trust as Moat** - Human connection with doctors, social proof, compliance-as-feature
- **AI as Companion** - Not just chatbot, but personalized health relationship
- **Privacy Enables Access** - Confidentiality breaks taboo barriers, especially in Tier 2
- **Menopause as Wedge** - Universal, urgent, under-served entry point
- **Tier 2 as Secret Weapon** - 3x faster adoption potential due to unmet need

### Session 4: Assumption Reversal - Challenge Your Thinking
**Duration:** 15 minutes
**Description:** Identify core assumptions and flip them to uncover hidden risks and opportunities

#### Assumptions Challenged:

**Assumption #1: "Women in Tier 2 will trust digital platforms"**

**Reversal Analysis:**

**Failure Modes:**
- Digital divide: Limited phone access or inadequate data
- Privacy invasion: Family members checking phones

**Mitigations:**
1. App hiding feature (disguised icon/name)
2. Offline mode for low data environments
3. Future: Physical partnerships in Tier 2/3 cities (clinics, pharmacies as touchpoints)

**Assumption #2: "Doctors will prescribe HRT/GLP-1 via teleconsultation"**

**Critical Regulatory Risk Identified:**

**Current State:**
- No preliminary research on Indian telemedicine guidelines completed yet
- Unclear which medications may require in-person consultations
- Potential existential threat if government restricts telemedicine prescriptions

**Doctor Protection Needs:**
- All government approvals/certifications displayed prominently
- Clear compliance framework

**FLAGGED FOR CRITICAL RESEARCH PHASE:** Regulatory compliance investigation needed before launch

**Strategic Options to Consider:**

**Option A: Conservative Launch (Lower Risk)**
- Require in-person first consultation at partner clinics
- Telemedicine for follow-ups only
- Slower growth but bulletproof compliance

**Option B: Phased Approach (Balanced)**
- Launch with lifestyle/diagnostics/community first
- Add prescriptions once regulatory path 100% clear

**Option C: Aggressive Launch (Higher Risk)**
- Full telemedicine prescriptions from day 1
- Legal team ensures current compliance
- Rapid acquisition but potential scrutiny

**Option D: Hybrid Model**
- Partner with established platforms (Practo, 1mg)
- Focus on hormonal health experience layer
- They handle prescription compliance

**Assumption #3: "Women will pay ‚Çπ999-‚Çπ2,999/month subscription"**

**Pricing Strategy:**

**Alternative Models:**
1. Monthly subscription: ‚Çπ999-‚Çπ2,999
2. Pay-per-consultation (if subscription is barrier)
3. Corporate wellness partnerships (subsidized)
4. Diagnostic bundles: ‚Çπ2,500-‚Çπ8,000

**Free Trial Strategy:**
- 1 week full access to hook users
- No perpetual freemium tier
- Convert through trial experience

#### Insights Discovered:
- Regulatory compliance is THE critical path item - cannot launch without clarity
- Tier 2 digital barriers solvable with creative product features
- Pricing flexibility important but secondary to proving value
