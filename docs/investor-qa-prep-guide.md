## 1. BUSINESS MODEL & UNIT ECONOMICS

### Q1: "Your LTV:CAC of 7.5x seems optimistic. How did you calculate this?"

**Answer:**
"Great question. Our LTV of ₹30,000 is based on conservative assumptions:
- Average subscription: 8.2 months at ₹3,500/month = ₹28,700
- Additional revenue: Initial consultation (₹450), diagnostics (₹450), add-ons (₹3,000)
- Total: ₹30,000+ per patient

Our CAC of ₹4,000 in Year 1 breaks down as:
- 40% digital ads (₹1,600)
- 30% doctor referrals (₹1,200)
- 20% content/SEO (₹800)
- 10% local events (₹400)

This gives us 7.5x LTV:CAC. By Year 3, CAC drops to ₹1,400 (referrals, brand) while LTV increases to ₹35,000+ (better retention). We have the full model in our appendix."

**If pressed:** "Industry benchmarks: HealthifyMe has 3-4x, Practo has 2-3x. Our advantage is subscription model (not transactional) + doctor relationships (switching costs)."

### Q2: "8.2 months average subscription seems short. Why not longer retention?"

However, we're building for 12+ month retention through:

By Year 3, we target 12-month average, which increases LTV to ₹42,000."

### Q9: "Your revenue projections show ₹60L in Year 1 from 1,000 users. That's only ₹6,000/user/year, not ₹30K LTV. Explain."

**Answer:**
"Excellent observation. The ₹6,000 is *average* revenue per user in Year 1 due to **cohort ramping**, not full LTV.

Here's why:
- User #1 signs up Month 1 → contributes 12 months revenue (₹42K)
- User #500 signs up Month 6 → contributes 6 months revenue (₹21K)
- User #1,000 signs up Month 12 → contributes 1 month revenue (₹3.5K)

**Average across all 1,000 users:**
- (₹42K × 200 early users) + (₹21K × 400 mid-year) + (₹10.5K × 400 late-year) ÷ 1,000
- = ₹60L total revenue
- = ₹6,000 per user average in Year 1

**By Year 2-3**, all users are active for full year, so revenue/user approaches LTV (₹30K). This is standard SaaS math—cohort ramping suppresses Year 1 revenue."

**Visual:** "If you look at our appendix, we have cohort analysis showing Month 1-12 revenue build-up."

**Risk 4: State-level telemedicine licensing**
- **Requirement:** Some states require separate licenses
- **Mitigation:** We obtain licenses in each state we operate (AP, Telangana, Karnataka initially)

**Overall risk level:** MODERATE (regulations evolving, but manageable with legal counsel on retainer)."
